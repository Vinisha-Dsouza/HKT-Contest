About the Contest

The HKT National Contest is a prestigious competition that brings together the brightest minds from across the country to compete in various technical domains.

This year's contest features multiple categories including coding challenges, technical quizzes, and innovative project presentations. Participants will have the opportunity to showcase their skills, learn from industry experts, and network with like-minded individuals.

Whether you're a beginner looking to gain experience or an advanced participant aiming for the top prizes, this contest offers something for everyone in the technical community.

Contest Timeline

June 1 - July 15, 2025

Registration Period

Participants can register for the contest through our online portal. Early registrations get access to preparatory materials.

July 20, 2025

Preliminary Round

Online screening test to shortlist candidates for the main competition. This will test basic technical knowledge.

August 10, 2025

Main Competition

Selected participants will compete in their chosen categories through practical challenges and presentations.

August 25, 2025

Results Announcement

Winners will be announced on our website and through email notifications. Prizes will be distributed within 15 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

The contest is open to all students (college and university) as well as young professionals aged 18-28. There are no restrictions based on academic discipline, though the challenges are technical in nature.

No, there is absolutely no registration fee for participating in the HKT National Contest. We believe in providing equal opportunities to all talented individuals regardless of their financial background.

After registration, participants will receive access to preparatory materials including practice problems, sample questions from previous years, and recommended reading lists. We also conduct free webinars in the weeks leading up to the contest.

Winners are selected through a rigorous evaluation process by our panel of industry experts. Criteria include technical accuracy, innovation, problem-solving approach, and presentation skills (for project categories). All judging is done anonymously to ensure fairness.
